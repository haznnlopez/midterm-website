Amsterdam, Netherlands

I need to be in Amsterdam because I’m tired of life feeling like a straight line when it should feel like a winding, emerald-colored canal. I want to be the one pedaling a vintage bicycle over a cobblestone bridge at sunrise, the "cling-cling" of my bell echoing against 17th-century tilted houses that look like they were pulled straight from a storybook. I’m desperate to lose myself in the Jordaan, ducking into a candlelit "Brown Café" where the walls are stained with centuries of secrets and the air smells like spiced apple pie and roasted coffee. I need to stand in the Rijksmuseum, face-to-face with the massive, moody genius of Rembrandt’s Night Watch, and then feel the world tilt as I swing out over the city’s edge at the A’DAM Lookout. I want to glide through the water on a wooden boat at twilight, watching the bridges light up like strings of pearls reflecting in the ripples. I need to walk through the silent, hallowed halls of the Anne Frank House to remember what matters, and then find myself surrounded by a million blooming tulips that prove life is a riot of color. I don't just want a vacation; I need to breathe in the freedom, the art, and the gezelligheid—that cozy, golden Dutch magic—until I finally understand what it’s like to live in a city that turned water into a masterpiece.
