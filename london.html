London, UK

I need to be in London because I refuse to go another day without hearing the deep, resonant chime of Big Ben vibrating through my chest as I stand on Westminster Bridge. I want to be the one ducking into a red-tiled Underground station, feeling that sudden rush of warm, metallic wind that signals the arrival of a train destined for somewhere legendary. I’m desperate to lose myself in the steam and spice of Borough Market, following the scent of roasting coffee and freshly baked flour until I’m holding a warm salt beef bagel in my hands while the city thrums around me. I have to stand at the edge of the Thames at twilight, watching the lights of Tower Bridge reflect in the dark, swirling water like a trail of spilled ink, and I need to feel the hallowed, heavy silence of Westminster Abbey, where the history of a thousand years is etched into the very stone beneath my feet. I want to spend a rainy afternoon tucked into a velvet booth of a centuries-old pub in Soho, the air thick with the murmur of a hundred different accents and the comforting clink of pint glasses. I don’t just want to see the skyline; I want to climb to the top of St. Paul’s Cathedral and see the "Big Smoke" stretch out before me, a chaotic, beautiful masterpiece of ancient spires and glass giants. I need to go because London isn’t just a city—it’s a living, breathing epic, and I am ready to finally step into its pages.
