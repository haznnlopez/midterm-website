New York City, USA

I need to be in New York City because I refuse to spend another year watching my life play out in a minor key while the rest of the world is screaming in technicolor. I want to be the one standing at the edge of the SUMMIT One Vanderbilt glass floor, suspended 1,100 feet above the pavement, feeling that heart-stopping rush as the city grid pulses beneath my boots like a living circuit board. I’m desperate to lose myself in the neon-soaked fever dream of Times Square at midnight, where the air hums with the energy of eight million stories, and then disappear into a velvet-walled jazz club in the Village where the saxophone feels like it’s speaking only to me. I have to walk across the Brooklyn Bridge at sunrise, watching the skyline transform into a crown of diamonds, and I need to taste that first, life-changing bite of a corner-store bagel that makes every other meal I’ve ever had feel like a rehearsal. I want to sit on the steps of The Met and feel like the protagonist of my own grand adventure, surrounded by five thousand years of human genius. I don’t just want to visit; I need to breathe in the ambition, the grit, and the relentless beauty of this place until I finally feel as big and as bold as the city itself. I belong where the lights never dim and the possibilities never end—I need to finally wake up in the city that never sleeps.
