A Day in My Life with AI

By Amir Jo-hanz B. Lopez | January 15, 2026

Instead of starting my day with a sip of coffee, I start it with lingering questions in mind.

"What should I do for today?"
"What should I prioritize?"
"How can I do this or that?"

Amidst these mind-boggling thoughts that clouded my brain came my hero who helped me in deciding with ease. Here is how AI tools and software gradually started to become part of my routine.

As I go through my day, these tools have become my second brain. At first, it was beneficial for me since I tend to overthink a lot. Having this sense of guidance and assurance helped me in navigating through my day. Some instances where I use AI is whenever I get stuck with complex, complicated, and confusing homeworks. I have always sought these tools' assistance. I also tend to rant to these tools when I feel overwhelmed with emotions. In some way, these tools were like mentors, therapists, and even doctors, which I took advantage due to their accessibility.

When Help Becomes A Habit

As much as I enjoy and appreciate the benefits of using AI tools, there are times when the line between using them merely as tools and letting it decide for you start to blur. It is true that at this age and time, AI is unavoidable and there's no choice for us but to embrace it. However, there should always be boundaries.

Whenever I use AI for assistance, I feel productive and sure of what I'm doing. But, when I use it to make decisions for me, something in me shifts. Sure, decisions feel easier, but it also feels less mine. These tools view our prompts from an objective standpoint and doesn't look at what's beyond. This is not about how AI gives bad advice, it is about how I feel less in-charge when it comes to my judgment. And this is important for our growth. Growth comes with mistakes, uncertainty, and reflection—something no algorithm can replicate.

In the end, this is still a part of my growth. AI tools are there for me to amplify my thoughts. They are there for me to assist me. But they should never replicate and replace my voice. As we go on with our lives, it is important to know and internalize that our choices and decisions are ours. Now that you're done reading this, my title could use a little tweaking. Changing it to: "A Day in My Life with Me and AI" now emphasizes my position, my stance, and my presence in my daily routine with AI as a mere companion.
