Paris, France

I need to be in Paris because I refuse to go another day without feeling that specific, golden ache of a sunset over the Seine. I want to be the one sitting at a tiny, marble-topped bistro table, watching the world drift by with a glass of wine in my hand and the smell of fresh baguettes drifting through the air. I have to see the Eiffel Tower sparkle against a velvet sky for the first time with my own eyes—not through a screen—and I need to lose myself in the winding, cobblestone secrets of Montmartre where the ghosts of artists still linger. I’m desperate to stand in the shadow of the Louvre and feel the weight of centuries of beauty pressing down on me, to finally understand what it means to truly savor a life lived slowly and elegantly. I don't just want to visit; I need to breathe in that city until I’m convinced that romance isn't dead and that I’m exactly where I was always meant to be.
